🏦 Credit Risk and Loan Approval Analysis

## 📌 About the Project

This project is an end-to-end credit risk and loan approval analysis using Microsoft Excel, PostgreSQL, SQL, and Looker Studio.

The objective was to analyze loan applications, identify factors affecting loan approvals, evaluate customer creditworthiness, and uncover risk patterns through SQL analysis and an interactive dashboard.

The project follows a complete analytics workflow—from data cleaning in Excel, SQL analysis in PostgreSQL, to creating an interactive dashboard in Looker Studio that enables users to explore loan approval trends, customer risk levels, CIBIL score distribution, and lending patterns.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- Analyze overall loan application performance.
- Evaluate loan approval and rejection rates.
- Identify the impact of CIBIL score on loan approvals.
- Analyze customer risk levels.
- Compare average loan amounts across different risk categories.
- Study the relationship between annual income and loan amount.
- Understand the effect of self-employment on loan approval decisions.
- Build an interactive dashboard for business insights.

## 📈 Key Insights

- Approximately 62% of loan applications were approved.
- Applicants with Excellent and Good CIBIL scores had significantly higher approval rates than applicants with Poor credit scores.
- Medium Risk customers represented the largest portion of loan applicants.
- Higher annual income generally corresponded to larger loan amounts.
- High Risk applicants received the highest average loan amount, while Medium Risk applicants received comparatively lower loan amounts.
- Self-employed and non-self-employed applicants showed an almost equal distribution.
